Case study

Revel Rideshare Tablet

A feature-rich tablet installed in the backseat of a Tesla rideshare vehicle that offers a passenger a more premium trip experience.

Overview

In spring of 2021 Revel launched its rideshare program with a fleet of signature-blue Tesla Model Ys. As a way to differentiate their program from competitors, a consumer-facing tablet was installed in every backseat. I led design on the tablet’s interface and collaborated closely with a small team of engineers and product stakeholders.

Summary
Problem

The rideshare market is saturated with carbon copy competition. How does a new entry into the space differentiate itself from the rest of the ridsharing landscape?

Solution

Offer a unique, premium experience by installing a dedicated tablet in the backseat of each vehicle giving the passenger an interface through which they can obtain info or configure their journey.

My Role

Worked with product manager and engineers. Led design, prototyping, and on-the-fly problem solving

Initial Work

We started with a blue sky vision and and whittled down untenable features as we iterated.

A lot of time was devoted to collaborating with engineering stakeholers to figure out a seemless login as well as a vehicle pairing process

We also did several deep dives researching media player options (Spotify, Pandora, radio?)

Music player iteration

The music player went through several iterations as we explored different music providers. We eventually landed on a pared down, simple UI for the initial release with the intent of enriching it with subsequent iterations.

Music Selection

A major determining factor through the process was leaning about third party music providers. We assumed initially we’d go through a service like Spotify, however, since we were pressed with many operational hurdles and an aggressive deadline our lead engineer made right recommendation to use free streaming radio feeds for our music choices.

The weather widget

We built an ad hoc weather widget for the tablet to offer a subtle touch on-brand experience. It was a great opportunity to create a fun set of weather icons.

In the hot seat

The seat warmer control went through a suprisingly high number of iterations. For the initial release we overlaid the heating controls on top of an image of the back row of seats in the hopes that the passenger will visually intuit which button controls which seat.

Results

The passenger tablet provided an engaging value proposition and memorable product distinction. The tablet could have benefited from taking more time to QA before our initial release, however, that was a compromise we consciously made to meet an aggressive deadline.


Ultimately, due to regulatory circumstances out of our control, we were forced to sunset the tablet. However, during its brief tenure, the tablet generated high engagement numbers and, I believe, could have proven to be a very valuable customer touchpoint had it had more time to mature.
